# pyRAM transmission loss, Kerguelen2015 summer, 25 Hz

Transmission loss computed with pyRAM (parabolic equation model) on
GLORYS12V1 sound speed profiles, along 24 radial transects out to 1000
km, for the South Kerguelen Plateau (SKP) mooring site. Used by the
`noiseLevels` article to demonstrate `pDetGivenNL` against real
propagation physics rather than spherical spreading.

## Usage

``` r
kerguelen2015TL
```

## Format

A data.frame with 10,000 rows and 25 columns:

- range_m:

  Range in metres, 100 to 1,000,000 in 100 m steps.

- tl000, tl015, ..., tl345:

  Transmission loss in dB at that range, one column per transect, named
  by azimuth in degrees.

## Source

## Details

Eight of the 24 transects hit land or leave the modelled bathymetry well
before 1000 km (see
[kerguelen2015TLmeta](https://briansmiller.github.io/callDensity/reference/kerguelen2015TLmeta.md)).
Rows beyond a given transect's `TruncationDistance_m` describe whatever
pyRAM computed past that point, not open-water propagation, and should
not be used without masking via `truncationDistance`.
